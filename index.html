Ayrton Senna

The man who brought Brazil to the podium and inspired a nation

  • 1960 - Ayrton Senna da Silva is born in SÃ£o Paulo, Brazil.
  • 1973 - Begins his karting career, showing exceptional talent from an early age.
  • 1977 - Wins the South American Kart Championship.
  • 1981 -
  • Moves to England to compete in Formula Ford races, winning two championships in his first year.
  • 1982 -
  • Wins the British Formula 3 Championship and the Macau Grand Prix, attracting the attention of Formula 1 teams.
  • 1984 -
  • Makes his Formula 1 debut with the Toleman team at the Brazilian Grand Prix. At the Monaco GP, in wet conditions, he puts on a spectacular performance, finishing 2nd.
  • 1985 - Signs with Lotus and secures his first Formula 1 victory at the Portuguese Grand Prix, in torrential rain.
  • 1988 - Transfers to McLaren and begins a legendary rivalry with Alain Prost. Wins his first Formula 1 World Championship title.
  • 1990 - Wins his second Formula 1 World Championship, in a high-tension season.
  • 1991 - Secures his third and final Formula 1 World Championship title, becoming one of the most successful drivers in history.
  • 1993 - Wins the European Grand Prix at Donington Park, in a memorable wet race, considered one of his greatest performances.
  • 1994 -
  • Moves to the Williams team. On May 1st, during the San Marino Grand Prix at Imola, he suffers a fatal accident at the Tamburello corner, at the age of 34.
  • After 1994 - His legacy transcends the sport, inspiring millions of people in Brazil and worldwide. The Ayrton Senna Institute is founded by his sister Viviane Senna, aiming to develop the potential of Brazilian children and youth through education.
